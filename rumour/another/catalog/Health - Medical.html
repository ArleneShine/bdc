          7 Times the Internet Gave You Terrible Medical Advice
          When the Internet is wrong, it's really, really wrong. Here are seven examples of the worst medical advice ever given out online.
          Can You Catch AIDS From a Pineapple?
          Internet rumor claims a 10-year-old boy came down with AIDS after eating pineapple from a roadside vendor who was infected with HIV.
          Is It True that Poinsettias Are Poisonous to Pets?
          Is it true that poinsettia plants are poisonous if ingested, especially by small children and pets?
          Is It True That Asparagus Can Cure Cancer?
          Viral message citing alleged cancer expert 'Richard R. Vensal, D.D.S.' purports to prove that asparagus prevents and/or cures cancer.
          Can the Heat From a BBQ Really Melt Your Contacts?
          Claim: The heat from a backyard barbecue can melt plastic contact lenses and fuse them to the eye, causing severe injury and blindness.
          Do Plastic Water Bottles Really Cause Cancer?
          Bottled water cancer danger? Viral text warns against drinking bottled water left in a warm car because the heat may cause cancer-producing toxins to 'leak' from the plastic into the water.
          Return of an Urban Legend: The Blue Star LSD Tattoo
          The urban legend of the 'Blue Star LSD tattoo' -- a transferable decal for children allegedly spiked with LSD -- is back to cause another round of hysteria.
          Can This Photo of a Larvae Infestation Possibly Be Real?
          A viral image circulating online since the early 2000s purports to document a larvae infestation of the human breast.
          The Legend of the Cadaver's Arm
          Students in a medical school play a prank on a fellow student using the arm of a cadaver. It doesn't end well.
          Was This 'Cancer Update' from Johns Hopkins Real?
          Viral text touting 'alternative' cancer treatments is falsely attributed to Johns Hopkins University.
          Watch Doctors Pull a Live Worm Out of This Patient's Eye!
          Viral images purportedly show doctors removing a live worm from a patient's eye. Are they real or fake?
          Will a Copper Penny Cure a Bee Sting?
          Viral message claims that taping a copper penny over a bee sting (or hornet sting) will provide overnight relief from redness and swelling.
          Did a Woman Really Lick Cockroach Eggs off an Envelope?
          Urban Legend: A woman finds cockroach larvae inside her tongue after licking an envelope infested with cockroach eggs.
          What Happened to the Girl Who Ate Cockroach Eggs at Taco bell?
          What? A woman got sick after eating a taco or burrito infested with cockroach eggs?? Calm down, it's only an urban legend!
          Is There a Nasty Secret Ingredient in Your Energy Drink?
          We look into the claim that the secret ingredient responsible for the boost you get from Red Bull and other energy drinks comes from bull sperm.
          Beware of Calls from These "Killer Phone Numbers"
          Forwarded emails and text messages warn mobile phone users not to accept calls from certain numbers which allegedly transmit a high-frequency signal causing brain hemorrhage and death.
          Are Raw Egg Whites a Legitimate Burn Remedy?
          Egg whites as a burn remedy? Viral text touts coating burned skin with raw egg whites as a 'miracle healing' home remedy. Doctors not so much.
          Read About South Korea's Bizarre Fear of "Fan Death"
          Is it true that people have died simply because they slept in a closed room with the fan running all night?
          Did a Study Say Fellatio Decreases Risk of Breast Cancer in Women?
          Homemade web page masquerading as a CNN news story claims women can decrease their risk of breast cancer by "performing the act of fellatio on a regular basis."
          Creepy, Scary Babysitting Experience
          Keri is freaked out by scary disturbances from outside the house while babysitting.
          About the US Environmental Protection Agency the EPA
          Since 1970, the EPA has sett and enforced standards to safeguard the land, air and water as well as protect human health.
          Does Flour Really Work as a Burn Remedy?
          No. Plain white flour will not stop pain from a burn nor promote healing. Experts say to use cool water and cover the burn loosely with sterile gauze.
          "Hair of the Dog" and Other Hangover "Cures"
          Hangover cures and folk remedies: herewith, a generous pouring of "hair of the dog" in anticipation of New Year's Eve and its aftermath. Caveat potor!
          Is a Dog's Mouth Cleaner Than a Human's?

          Hand Sanitizer Hazardous to Children

          Leftover Onions Are Poisonous

          Smoking Bed Bugs to Get High

          Rat Urine on Soda Pop Cans Is Lethal

          Vicks VapoRub on Soles of Feet Relieves Coughing
